About eSIM

What is the eSIM?

eSIM (aka embedded SIM) is a small chip that replaces the physical SIM card (aka not embedded SIM) in your smartphone, tablet, smartwatch or other compatible device.

Its built right into that compatible device, ready and waiting to be activated on a mobile network in the grand battle of eSIM vs physical SIM. You might also hear names like electronic SIM and software SIM and rewritable SIM card. But were sticking with eSIM.

What phones are compatible with eSIM?

Most phones manufactured after 2020 come with eSIM.

This includes the latest devices from Apple, Samsung and Google are compatible with eSIM, plus it's supported by some handsets from the likes of Huawei, Honor, Motorola, Oppo and Sony.

But don't worry, we'll will check your device's compatibility as you step through the download process.

How do I switch my physical SIM to an eSIM?

With all things eSIM, your phone needs to be compatible.

Then all you need to do is jump onto the My Boost Mobile app, and there two different ways to do it within the app.

Services tab

  1. On the services homepage, tap the service you want to switch
  2. Scroll down to the bottom and tap Transfer or restore eSIM.
  3. Follow the prompts and you'll be binning your SIM card in no time.

Get help

  1. Tap Get help and select your service number
  2. Select set up or manage eSIM
  3. From here, we'll guide you through the process and you'll be physical SIM-less in a few minutes.

Do I need to change my plan, my mobile number or lose my credit if I switch to eSIM?

No. Switching from a physical SIM to an eSIM doesnt impact your plan, mobile number or prepaid credit. Youll be on the same mobile network as before.

How do I fix an issue with my service on eSIM?

If youre having problems with eSIM setup, eSIM service, eSIM profile or eSIM usage, simply search eSIM on our help page.

Can't find what you're looking for? Our Customer Care team will help solve the problem. You can message us or call them on 1800 100 933.

Does the Boost Mobile eSIM work with my smartwatch?

At the moment, Boost Mobile eSIM is not available for smartwatches.

What is a dual SIM?

A dual SIM device can support two SIM cards. Depending on the device, this could be two physical SIMs, two eSIMs or a combination of both.

This means you can make and receive calls and texts with
two different mobile numbers, as well as use mobile data from two separate plans. And just like twins, the plans dont even need to be identical.

Dual SIM functionality differs between device models. Check with the device manufacturer for details.

What is the difference between dual SIM and eSIM?

eSIM is digital SIM technology available on compatible devices that allows you to connect without requiring a physical SIM card.

Dual SIM support two mobile services at the same time. Some dual SIM devices also come with eSIM tech, but not all.

Does eSIM work without internet?

To get started, youll have to download your carriers profile, so youll need an internet connection there.

But once thats done, and the eSIM is activated, it works the same way as a physical SIM card. (So long story short, you only need the internet for that first bit. Then youre good to go.)

How does choosing eSIM help the environment?

Using an eSIM can eliminate the CO2 emissions involved in manufacturing, warehousing, and delivery of a physical plastic SIM.

Is eSIM available in Australia?

Yup yup! All you have to do to sign up for your eSIM service is download the My Boost Mobile app and select Buy a prepaid plan on eSIM.and follow the prompts. Its pretty straightforward though, tbh.

Is eSIM better than physical SIM Australia?

eSIM is like so much better. You can activate, and get connected in a flash.

You can manage multiple numbers and data plans on a single device without swapping SIM cards. . You dont have to worry about losing SIMs, or putting them in that little tray the wrong way up, or getting fingerprints on the bronze bit even though that probably wouldnt make a difference to your bars, but better safe than sorry. Oh, and theres less chance of accidentally swallowing one than there is with a physical SIM.
